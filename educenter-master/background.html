Our History and Foundation

Northern Uganda - Our Operational Region

Understanding the region that shapes our mission and drives our commitment to community transformation

Geography & Location

Northern Uganda is geographically situated in the northern part of Uganda, bordering South Sudan to the north and Kenya to the east. It covers several administrative districts, including Gulu, Kitgum, Lira, Pader, Arua, Nebbi, and many others.

The region is characterized by vast savannahs, rivers, and lakes such as Lake Albert, Kyoga and Kwania. The White Nile River flows through this region, contributing to its agricultural potential and natural beauty.

Map of Uganda Showing Sub Regions

The Lango Sub Region (highlighted area)

Climate

Northern Uganda has a tropical climate, with two distinct seasons: a dry season and a rainy season.

  • Rainy Season: March to November (peak: April-June, August-October)
  • Dry Season: December-February and June-July
  • Agriculture: Climate supports subsistence farming as primary economic activity
Lango Sub Region Map

Detailed view of our primary operational area

Culture & Ethnic Groups

The region is culturally diverse, home to various ethnic groups, including the Acholi, Lango, Madi, Alur, Kakwa, and others.

Acholi People

Residing mainly in Gulu, Kitgum, and Pader districts. Known for rich oral traditions, dances, and cultural rituals.

Lango People

Predominantly found in Lira and neighbouring districts. Known for communal lifestyle and practices.

Traditional dances, storytelling, and music are integral to the social fabric of these communities, with cultural events and gatherings being a common part of life.

Economy

The economy of Northern Uganda is primarily based on agriculture and livestock.

Agriculture: Millet, sorghum, beans, maize, and cassava
Livestock: Cattle, goats, and poultry farming
Cash Crops: Simsim (sesame), shea butter, and groundnuts
Diversification: Trade, tourism, and small-scale industries

Historical Context

The LRA Conflict (Late 1980s - Mid 2000s)

Northern Uganda has a complex history, notably impacted by the two-decade-long insurgency led by the Lord's Resistance Army (LRA) under Joseph Kony.

  • Loss of life and displacement of populations
  • Destruction of infrastructure
  • Many displaced into IDP camps
  • Deep social and economic scars
Recovery & Rebuilding (Mid 2000s - Present)

Since the mid-2000s, the region has experienced relative peace with focused rebuilding efforts.

  • Rebuilding communities and infrastructure
  • Reintegration of former child soldiers
  • Support for displaced people
  • Economic recovery initiatives

Blessed Jildo and Daudi Livelihood Transformation Initiative

Who We Are

Blessed Jildo and Daudi is an indigenous (NGO) operating in Northern Uganda with its office based in Lira City West division. It is registered and certified by the Uganda Registration Service Bureau with the purpose of promoting and offering comprehensive initiative programmes geared towards enhancing sustainable development and livelihood transformation.

About Us

At Blessed Jildo and Daudi Livelihood Transformation Initiative, we create transformed communities through concerted interventions that mitigate the impact of poverty, climate change, drug addictions, disease, promote inclusive and sustainable development that empowers them to personally access markets for a transformed livelihood.

Our Focus

We empower youth, children, orphans, vulnerable women, disabled persons, elderly persons, street children, school dropouts, drug addicts, child mothers and other vulnerable groups by creating initiatives that impart basic life skills through vocational and improved farming practices for sustainable livelihood.

Environmental Commitment

We engage communities on environmental protection and conservation of natural resources to ensure sustainable development for future generations.

Our Philosophy

Community Leadership

We identify committed child mothers, men, and youth leaders from among the community for planning and implementing sustainable development programs.

Community Participation

We involve community participation in each and every step of the sustainable development programs.

Self-Reliance

We jointly facilitate the development of vulnerable communities to attain self-reliance.
